Aa

Phonetics highlights

French uses the same 26 letters, but accents and nasal vowels change sounds.

éèêë àâîï ùûüç œæ
an/en nasal "ɑ̃" France, vent
in/ain nasal "ɛ̃" vin, main
on nasal "ɔ̃" bon, pont
ou like "oo" vous, tout

Silent finals: most French final consonants are not pronounced — vous sounds like "voo", not "vooz". Trust the vowel, drop the ending.

Language levels

Six CEFR levels

The Common European Framework describes six proficiency levels — find where you fit before starting the maze.

A1

Débutant

Beginner

  • Introduce yourself and ask basic personal questions
  • Understand familiar words and very basic phrases
  • Interact simply when the other person speaks slowly
A2

Élémentaire

Elementary

  • Describe your background and immediate environment
  • Communicate in simple, routine tasks
  • Use basic expressions about familiar topics
B1

Intermédiaire

Intermediate

  • Deal with most travel situations in French-speaking areas
  • Produce connected text on familiar topics
  • Describe experiences, events, and give brief reasons
B2

Avancé

Upper-Intermediate

  • Understand the main ideas of complex text
  • Interact with fluency and spontaneity
  • Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects
C1

Autonome

Advanced

  • Understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts
  • Express ideas fluently without searching for words
  • Use language flexibly for academic and professional purposes
C2

Maîtrise

Mastery

  • Understand virtually everything heard or read
  • Summarize information from spoken and written sources
  • Express yourself spontaneously, fluently, and precisely
